# Academic Research: Cryptocurrency Market Microstructure

## Paper 1: Cryptocurrency Market Microstructure - A Systematic Literature Review

**Authors:** José Almeida & Tiago Cruz Gonçalves
**Journal:** Annals of Operations Research, Volume 332, pages 1035-1068 (2024)
**Published:** October 27, 2023
**Citations:** 25+ (as of Dec 2025)
**URL:** https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s10479-023-05627-5

### Key Findings from Systematic Review (138 research papers analyzed)

The academic literature on cryptocurrency market microstructure presents heterogeneous conclusions:

**Market Efficiency:**
- Some studies indicate cryptocurrency market is inefficient (Akyildirim et al., 2021; Grobys et al., 2020; Sapkota & Grobys, 2021; Vidal-Tomás et al., 2019)
- Other studies suggest the opposite - market efficiency exists (Alvarez-Ramirez & Rodriguez, 2021; Burggraf & Rudolf, 2020; Caporale & Plastun, 2019)

**Market Interconnectedness:**
- Some research suggests cryptocurrency market is interconnected (Corbet et al., 2018; Huynh et al., 2018; Shahzad et al., 2021; Tiwari et al., 2020)
- Other studies suggest the contrary - limited interconnectedness (Kostika & Laopodis, 2020; Sifat et al., 2019)

**Cross-Asset Correlation:**
- Some studies find cryptocurrency market connected to other assets (Kalyvas et al., 2021; Kurka, 2019; Luu et al., 2020; Thampanya et al., 2020)
- Others suggest limited correlation with traditional assets (Corbet et al., 2018; Gil-Alana et al., 2020)

### Research Implications
- Cryptocurrency market characterized by rapid development
- Among fastest growing world financial markets
- Considered popular investment asset among investors
- High attention from media, regulators, institutional and individual investors
- Literature remains unconsolidated with heterogeneous conclusions
