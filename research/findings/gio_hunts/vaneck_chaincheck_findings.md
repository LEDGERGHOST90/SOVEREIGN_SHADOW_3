# VanEck Mid-December 2025 Bitcoin ChainCheck - Key Findings

**Source:** VanEck, December 22, 2025
**Authors:** Patrick Bush (Senior Investment Analyst), Matthew Sigel (Head of Digital Assets Research)

## GEO Framework Scores (as of Dec 2025)
- **Global Liquidity:** 7 (mixed but skews bullish)
- **Ecosystem Leverage:** 7 (low speculative energy - futures OI at $29B, lowest since Oct 2024)
- **Onchain Activity:** 3 (very weak - mempool data lowest since 2022)

## Key Metrics (30-day changes as of Dec 15, 2025)
- **BTC Price:** -9% (30-day volatility >45, highest since April 2025)
- **30-day RSI:** Bottomed at ~32 on Nov 22 when BTC hit ~$80.7k
- **Perpetual Futures Basis:** 5% annualized (down from year average of 7.4%)
- **Hash Rate:** -4% m/m (sharpest decline since April 2024)
- **Daily Fees:** -14% m/m (in dollar terms)
- **New Addresses:** -1% m/m (stagnating)

## Holder Behavior Analysis
### Medium-term holders SELLING:
- 1-2yr cohort: -900 bps m/m
- 2-3yr cohort: -1250 bps m/m
- 3-5yr cohort: -550 bps m/m

### Long-term holders HOLDING:
- 5-7yr cohort: +27 bps m/m
- 7-10yr cohort: -18 bps m/m
- >10yr cohort: +50 bps m/m

**Interpretation:** Short/medium-term cyclical players dumping while long-term Bitcoin bulls holding firm.

## Institutional Flows
- **DATs (Digital Asset Treasuries):** +42k BTC (+4% m/m) - largest since July 2025
- **Total DAT Holdings:** 1.09M BTC
- **Strategy (formerly MicroStrategy):** Bought 29.4k BTC of the 42k total
- **BTC ETPs:** -120 bps m/m (holdings dropped to 1.308M BTC)

## Mining Economics
- **Breakeven Electricity (S19 XP):** $0.077/kWh (Dec 2025) vs $0.12/kWh (Dec 2024)
- **Chinese Mining Shutdown:** 1.3 GW capacity in Xinjiang (~400k machines, up to 10% of network)
- **Hash Rate CAGR since 2020:** +62%

## Historical Hash Rate Signal (Bullish Contrarian)
- When 90-day hash rate growth is negative:
  - 180-day forward returns positive 77% of time
  - Average return: +72%
- When hash rate is positive:
  - 180-day forward returns positive 61% of time
  - Average return: +48%
- **Historical alpha from buying during hash rate compression:** +2400 bps

## Key Takeaways
1. Corporations buying dip while ETF investors retreat
2. Miner capitulation may signal bottom (historically bullish contrarian)
3. "Diamond hands" divergence - long-term holders unmoved while medium-term selling
4. Weak on-chain activity but improving liquidity conditions
5. Reset in speculative leverage points to cautious optimism
