# IMF Working Paper: The Crypto Cycle and US Monetary Policy

## Source: International Monetary Fund Working Paper WP/23/163
## Authors: Natasha Che, Alexander Copestake, Davide Furceri, and Tammaro Terracciano
## Published: August 2023
## Department: Asia and Pacific Department

### Abstract Summary

The paper examines fluctuations in crypto markets and their relationships to global equity markets and US monetary policy.

### Key Findings

1. **Single Price Component ("Crypto Factor"):** The authors identify a single price component that explains 80% of variation in crypto prices

2. **Increasing Equity Correlation:** The crypto factor's increasing correlation with equity markets coincided with the entry of institutional investors into crypto markets

3. **Fed Tightening Impact:** US Fed tightening reduces the crypto factor through the risk-taking channel - contrary to claims that crypto assets provide a hedge against market risk

4. **Spillover Risk:** A stylized heterogeneous-agent model with time-varying aggregate risk aversion can explain the empirical findings and highlights possible spillovers from crypto to equity markets if institutional investor participation ever became large

### Research Classification
- JEL Classification Numbers: E44; E52; F33
- Keywords: US Monetary Policy; Cryptoassets; Stock Markets

### Implications for Current Market

This IMF research provides important context for understanding:
- Why crypto markets have become more correlated with traditional risk assets
- The impact of Federal Reserve monetary policy on crypto valuations
- The role of institutional investors in changing crypto market dynamics
- Risk transmission channels between crypto and traditional financial markets

### Policy Relevance

The paper suggests that as institutional participation grows, crypto markets become less of an independent asset class and more integrated with traditional financial markets, making them susceptible to the same macroeconomic forces (particularly monetary policy) that affect equities.
